Jaya Narain

Sr. Research Scientist, Apple Health AI

I am a Senior Research Scientist at Apple Health AI, where I work on applied machine learning for health and accessibility. I work with topics and domains across these areas — most recently with sensor foundation models and atypical speech. I was a technical leader for the motion foundation model in Air Pods Pro 3 and also worked on machine learning for the Adaptive Voice Shortcuts accessibility. My research has also focused on affective computing with sensors and speech, and understanding the mechanics of LLM instruction-following.

I completed my PhD at the MIT Media Lab in the Fluid Interfaces Group. I was an Apple PhD Scholar in AI for Health & Wellness and an NSF Graduate Research Fellow. My doctoral research focused on modeling and human-centered design for communication tools with nonverbal vocalizations for communicators who did not use typical verbal speech. I co-founded the MIT Assistive Technology Hackathon (ATHack) in 2013 and led the program for eight years, which brought together engineers, designers, and people with disabilities to learn together and co-create assistive technology prototypes. I have an MS in Mechanical Engineering and a BS in Mechanical & Ocean Engineering from MIT.
